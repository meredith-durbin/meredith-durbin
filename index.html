Past Work

I graduated from Pomona College in May 2014 with a BA in astrophysics. My senior thesis was on the RR Lyrae period-luminosity relation in IRAC channels 1 and 2, supervised by Dr. Victoria Scowcroft at Carnegie Observatories and Dr. Alma Zook at Pomona. This work was presented as a poster at AAS224, and we are currently preparing it for publication; in the meantime, my thesis is available here, or you can read the condensed version on Astrobites.

In summer 2012 I worked with Dr. Alma Zook using the 1-meter telescope at JPL's Table Mountain Observatory to obtain optical blazar polarimetry data and characterize the telescope polarimeter; the results of this research were presented at AAS221.

I have a strong interest in diversity in the sciences; as an undergraduate I co-founded αLPhA ("Awesome Ladies in Physics and Astronomy") at Pomona College along with two other physics majors in the class of 2014. I'm also interested in science illustration and communication; I contributed several art pieces and designed publicity posters for Pomona's festival celebrating physics in the arts, PhysPhest, for two years.
