About Me

I'm currently a fourth year astrophysics major at Pomona College. My thesis work is with Carnegie Observatories on the mid-infrared period-luminosity relation for RR Lyrae variable stars. I'm hoping to work in industry for a few years before looking into pursuing an astrophysics PhD.

I'm particularly interested in science writing and the role of media in science communication, including the influence of popular media on perceptions of scientists and how that interplays with gender, racial, and queer issues.

I am co-founder of ALPhA ("Awesome Ladies in Physics and Astronomy") along with two other women in my physics class, and have previously mentored for the Claremont Colleges Queer/Questioning/Allied Mentor Program.

When not in the physics building, I can be found playing around with Photoshop (a skill I never intended to make actual use of) and pretending I can sing and dance.
